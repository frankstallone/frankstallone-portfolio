    Did you know...?
 

Like many great stories, Dragonball starts out faultless. But, as the story progresses, the writers often forget about the small yet significant points they stated earlier. Some attempted "cover-ups" of earlier errors can also led to more errors.

Dragonball Z tells of a Super Saiyan, who appears every 1000 years. Gokou is shown to be this Saiyan, so he is supposed to be the only one able to reach Super Saian level.

Unexplained: Later, Trunks, Vegeta, and Gohan are also able to reach Super Saiyan mode

To try to clarify this error, Movie 8 introduces Brolly, the Legendary Saiyan who apears every 1000 years.

Unexplained: It is shown that Gokou can indeed reach SSJ2 and SSJ3 mode, and that Gohan can reach SSJ2 mode too.

To try to cover up this error, another term is introduced- the legendary Saiyan who appears every 1000 years- and that is Gokou when he reaches Super Gokou 4 mode.

When Cell exploded, he regenerated from a single surviving brain cell.

Unexplained: Cell's entire upper body (including the head and thus the brain) was blown away in an episode, but he still was able regenerate (without a brain cell).

In the Boo Series, Gohan trained to become Mystic Gohan, a stage where he was able to become stronger than reaching Super Saiyan form. Therefore, he does not reach SSJ mode when he fights Boo.

Unexplained: Gohan reaches SSJ form once again in Dragonball GT.

  Origin
 

Dragonball was derived from the Chinese story Journey to the West:

Gokou was derived from the Monkey King himself.

Gokou's bow was also the weapon that the Monkey King used.

Gokou's flying nimbus was also a source of transportation for the Monkey King.

Oolong was derived from the pig disciple.

Yamcha was derived from the former bandit disciple.

Klilyn was derived from the holy Tang monk.

Tenshin-han was derived from the 3-eyed god.

Ox-King was derived from the Ox King/Demon.

The Ox-King's fire mountain was the same home the Ox King from Journey to the West lived on.

The fan that Master Roshi claimed to have spilled juice on was also deprived from this Chinese legend.

King Yamma's special fruit was a fruit of the heavens in the Chinese story.

The Namek race was based on snails and slugs. This explains why Nameks can regenerate lost limbs.
