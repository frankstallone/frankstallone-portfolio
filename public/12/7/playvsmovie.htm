William Shakespeare's play Hamlet, is one of the greatest plays ever written. With the advent of the film industry, the classics were perfect targets for this new entertainment media. However, movies do not allow the viewer to develop an opinion that is more their own than if they had read the book.
     After hearing and reading Hamlet, it appeared that Hamlet did not desire his mother in a sexual way. He just appeared to have a soft spot in his heart for his mother. However, after Mel Gibson's portrayal of Hamlet, Hamlet's desire for Gertrude was almost undeniable. The scene with Hamlet on top of Gertrude made Hamlet seem like he was on the verge of being a rapist (however, that would only be true if Gertrude did not seem to be consenting to this act, but that is another issue). The play allows for the viewer to come to his or her own conclusion, the movie made the viewer see the director's opinion on Hamlet's problem.
     The question of Hamlet's insanity was also less up to the viewer in the movie than in the play. Scenes where Hamlet was lurking in the shadows were shown in the movie rather than assumed as in the play. Hamlet was in ramparts lurking around in the movie, but in the play, one assumed this, or did not assume this, depending on their viewpoint on Hamlet's sanity. The movie showed Hamlet as sane, feigning insanity. The play left it up to the reader to decide.
     While the movie was excellently done, the movie took away the viewer's chance for choice. The viewer saw the director's view, not their own, however, that is the type of freedom one gives up when viewing movies. Mel Gibson portrayed Hamlet perfectly. He was able to go from sane to insane in a heartbeat; much like the Hamlet of the play is able to do. The play is always going to be better than any movie, but Mel Gibson in Hamlet was a very good jab at Hamlet.
