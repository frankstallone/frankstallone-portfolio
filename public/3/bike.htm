Definition of Freestyle BMX: Riding a 20' bike in these catigories...

Street:
Riding, the streets, consists of a lot of grinding and gapping, and other skillz, kind of a show off to people on the streets. > Now, bikes for street riding usually have somewhere between a 19' - 20' top tube (the basic and I think best way to catagorize bike frames).
Flatland:
This is H A R D. This is Tricks done on flatland, not on a curve, no on a slant, hockey courts and it takes 100% ballance and another %30 to remember trick names =) > These frames/bikes are usually 18' - '19, I think I have heard of a '17 somewhere but the smaller sized head tube has to do with being able to maneuver around the bike better, without having to be as tall, or having to lug around a longer frame.
Dirt Jumping:
My personal suggestion is to start off a dirt jumper early in ur freestyle years, IF you wanna be good at half pipes and ramps. You learn many tricks in the air which is something not easily done. > These frames/bikes are usually thiner then Street bikes but the sizing range from '19 - '21. The reason they can be thinner is because when you are trying to jump a good 10 - 20 foot gap in the dirt, lighter may get you across a little safer, and without having to look like Steve Austin.
Ramps:
 Ahh yes, good old ramps. If you don't know half pipes, quarter pipes, and plain old ramps fall into this catagory, I think I made this catigory up its more like street, but you don't find a half pipe anywhere in the streets so I'd figure, why not confuse you and make another section! > Heh, these frames/bikes are usually 19' - 20' top tubes, because it is said that they carry better in the air then a flatland bike/frame.
