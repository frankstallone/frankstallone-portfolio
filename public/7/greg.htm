Let's see. To start things off I just want to say that I dislike Shakespeare in general. Maybe this is because of the way that it has been presented to me or maybe I just do not care for the way that he writes. This, however is not the case with this play. For once I can actually say that I enjoyed something that Shakespeare wrote. I think that this is because of the way that it was taught to me. I usually find Shakespeare's works to very boring and drab, but this time I was able to get into the play and analyze the characters. I have come to the conclusion that Hamlet was the least of the insane of all the characters. On the other hand the most insane character in the entire play was Claudius. All of the other characters were not necessarily insane but rather disturbed in their own way. Hamlet was the smartest of all the characters. He was able to play with people's minds in order to find out what they were thinking and planning. Throughout the entire play Hamlet was able to know what people had in store for him. Hamlet knew what Claudius, Rosencrantz and Guildenstern, Laertes, and Polonius had planned for him and what they were trying to do with him. Hamlet was always skillful enough to find out, through his wit and cunning attitude, what people were thinking. Because Hamlet was able to see his father's ghost does not mean that he was crazy or insane. Rather, it means that he had the introspective mind that nobody else in play had and was able to realize and see things that only the most intelligent of people could see. Ophelia, On the other hand, was going off the deep end from the start of the play. She was crazy from the beginning. This was all proven when her father was murdered. It is at this point that Ophelia really shows the reader that she is wacked out. She begins to sing and hand out "flowers" which are actually bones. I mean she winds up dying because she drowned in a brook, the most shallow of all bodies of water except for maybe a puddle. All these things prove that she was a little bit out there. All of the other characters were crazy in their own way. They all had something that made them a little bit strange or delirious. I mean how could someone not be if they married their brother's wife after murdering him or taking bribes in order to spy on their best friend. They only character besides Hamlet that was not insane would be Horatio. He was always there for Hamlet. He never tried to plot anything against anyone and was nothing but a good friend. So this is what I thought of the play. It is not the most in depth critique but it is rather just the way that I felt after reading and analyzing it.
