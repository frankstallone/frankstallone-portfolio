Hamlet in my eyes is one of the best plays I have ever read. The movie does the book no harm, and even accentuates the supreme quality of Shakespeare and his writing mind. After my ear became accustomed to hearing Shakespeare's older style of writing the words echoed through my mind like a voice screaming inside of a large hollow cavern. I can still remember his words from Romeo and Juliet, so romantic, "tis the lark, the herald of the mor n;" but his overtones in Hamlet excite me; death, deception, incest, sexual feeling, action, excitement, and murder. What more could any teenager want in a movie or more rarely read books in this modern era. I must say that it was the ending that really enticed me and made this play a hit. It wasn't the kind of happy ending we are used to seeing. It was religiously ended happily. Hamlet went to Heaven, and his foes went to hell. They got their just punishments in the end, and Hamlet completed his weighty task. Shakespeare's Hamlet is a story so well written that the classic invocation of the gods could not even come close to the writings done after the invocation of Shakespeare's guidance. Jake and Dweebert give this play two thumbs up!
