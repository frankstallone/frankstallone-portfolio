King Hamlet is dead. His ghost is making house calls. Gerturde is sleeping with his brother in law Claudius. A week after The King dies, shes married to Claudius. Ophelia is becoming delusional, and her sex life isn't improving either. And in the midst of it all, lies Prince Hamlet. None of his actions are without foresight, except for the death of poor innocent Polonius. Hamlet felt absolutely mortified about the death of Polonius. Hamlet is so smart he confuses even his own conscious when it comes to Ophelia. Hamlet's relationship with Ophelia is going straight to hell, she's becoming quite the out-patient. The brook got the best of her. She never had a chance. Hamlet becomes a promising playwright, his target audience was definitely affected by his work. Claudius never saw it coming. Even though Hamlet's vengeance is strong, he restrains himself from disgracing the church, and his ticket to the good place. King Hamlet's ghost has got Hamlet dancing on his finger, Hamlet fulfills his father's wishes, but not without side effects. Hamlet's ending holds true to the Shakespearean form when all the main characters perish in an extravagant show of deceit. Laertes is on an ego based power trip demanding the throne from Claudius. Laertes, having a weak mind, is pulled in by Claudius's smooth talking. Its too bad Claudius's smooth talking stabs him in the gut and tears out his insides. Having repented, Laertes is on his way to heaven with Gertrude. Gertrude perishes by Claudius's clever hand, the pearl meant for Hamlet was the culprit. Hamlet is dead, alas, and Horatio wants to go with him. The sweet prince denies him this opportunity, and turns all the lands owned by his father over to who else but good old Fortinbras. Fortinbras, a noble leader, ends the play with a powerful line, stating his belief in Hamlet's cleverness and his potential ability to rule as King.
